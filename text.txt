      An open scientific collaboration focused on neural regeneration and system-level brain repair.

    <h2>Purpose</h2>
    <p>
      The Open Neuroregeneration Initiative (NRI) is an independent, interdisciplinary effort aimed at understanding
      and enabling meaningful regeneration, repair, and integration of neural systems in the central nervous system.
    </p>

    <h2>Scope</h2>
    <p>
      NRI focuses on foundational and translational problems related to neural regeneration, stem cell–derived neural
      cells, circuit integration, neurovascular and neuroimmune interactions, and system-level recovery of brain function.
      Priority is given to bottlenecks whose resolution would unlock progress across multiple neurological conditions.
    </p>

    <h2>Approach</h2>
    <p>
      NRI emphasizes rigorous, mechanism-driven research, interdisciplinary collaboration, transparency in assumptions
      and methods, and adherence to ethical and safety standards. The initiative values incremental, verifiable advances
      over speculative claims.
    </p>

    <h2>Structure</h2>
    <p>
      NRI operates as an open, non-commercial scientific initiative composed of researchers and collaborators across
      institutions. Participation is based on contribution and shared scientific standards rather than hierarchy.
      Formal legal structures may be established in the future if required.
    </p>
